The Problem

Why Frugal IoT?

Typical IoT sensor

$200–$500

per sensor node, today

→

Our target

$20–$50

per sensor node, with Frugal IoT

At $200–$500 per sensor many critical problems simply cannot be addressed. Monitoring soil moisture across a smallholder farm, tracking water quality in a remote watershed, or measuring air quality in a school — none of these are viable when a single sensor costs as much as a month's wages. We believe the price point of $20–$50 per sensor unlocks a completely different set of solutions.

Our Approach

How We're Getting There

We believe that a successful solution at scale comes from developing a full stack of technologies that can be repurposed, adapted, and localized to solve specific problems.

Hardware

Open hardware designs for affordable sensor nodes, built for real-world field conditions.

Supports a wide range of sensors, actuators and development boards, see supported hardware.

Firmware

Features supported include: MQTT; Mesh networking; Buttons; Captive portal; OTA updates; OLED & LCD displays; Power management; etc

Server & Services

Scalable backend infrastructure with minimal running costs, deployable on modest hardware.

Includes: Downloadable data; Gateways to Google Sheets, Firebase etc;

Client

A multilingual phone and laptop client for data access, visualization, and device management. Including, graphing; live updates; administration interface.
